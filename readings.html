Selected Readings

(Partial list, in reverse chronological order.)

Creating Languages in Racket. Matthew Flatt. 2011. [Essay].

The Grid Book. Hannah B. Higgins. 2009. [Book].

The Faith of Graffiti. Norman Mailer, John Naar. [1974, 2009]. [Book].

Introduction to Automata Theory, Languages, and Computation, 3rd Edition. John E. Hopcroft, Rajeev Motwani, Jeffrey D. Ullman. 2007. [Book].

Compilers - Principles, Techniques and Tools (2nd Edition). Alfred W. Aho, Ravi Sethi, Jeffrey D. Ullman. 2006. [Book].
The first edition is quite good too, and can probably be found used.

Procedural Literacy: Educating the New Media Practitioner. Michael Mateas. 2005. [Essay].

Hacker's Delight. Henry S. Warren, Jr. 2003. [Book].
The link above is for the 2nd Edition, which I have not read but hear is quite good too.

Programming Languages as Operating Systems (or Revenge of the Son of the Lisp Machine). Matthew Flatt, Robert Bruce Findler, Shriram Krishnamurthi, Matthias Felleisen. 1999. [Essay]

Language Oriented Programming. M. P. Ward. 1994. [Essay].

Compiling with Continuations. Andrew W. Appel. 1992. [Book].

Languages of Art. Nelson Goodman. 1976. [Book].

Notes on the Synthesis of Form. Christopher Alexander. 1964. [Book].

LISP 1.5 Programmer's Manual. John McCarthy et al. 1962. [Book, PDF].

Course in General Linguistics. Ferdinand de Saussure. 1959. [Book].

Syntactic Structures, 1st Edition. Noam Chompsky. 1957. [Book].
