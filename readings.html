Selected Readings

(Partial list, in reverse chronological order.)

Visual Complexity - Mapping Patterns of Information. Manuel Lima. 2011. [Book]. There is a related web site at www.visualcomplexity.com, but the book provides extremely interesting and useful background on network visualization that does not appear to be available on the web.

Creating Languages in Racket. Matthew Flatt. 2011. [Essay].

The Grid Book. Hannah B. Higgins. 2009. [Book].

The Faith of Graffiti. Norman Mailer, John Naar. 2009, 1974. [Book].

Introduction to Automata Theory, Languages, and Computation, 3rd Edition. John E. Hopcroft, Rajeev Motwani, Jeffrey D. Ullman. 2007. [Book].

Persuasive Games. The Expressive Power of Videogames. Ian Bogost. 2007. [Book].

Processing - A Programming Handbook for Visual Designers and Artists. Casey Reas, Ben Fry. 2007. [Book].

Compilers - Principles, Techniques and Tools (2nd Edition). Alfred W. Aho, Ravi Sethi, Jeffrey D. Ullman. 2006. [Book]. The first edition is quite good too, and can probably be found used.

Procedural Literacy: Educating the New Media Practitioner. Michael Mateas. 2005. [Essay].

Creative Code. John Maeda. 2004. [Book].

Hacker's Delight. Henry S. Warren, Jr. 2003. [Book]. The link above is for the 2nd Edition, which I have not read but hear is quite good too.

Drawing on the Right Side of the Brain. Betty Edwards. 1999. [Book]. I took an excellent one week drawing class from the author's son, Brian Bomeisler. I highly recommend it for anyone who is interested in learning to draw, which means learning to see and record spacial relationships. Here is a link to Brian's web site: www.drawright.com.

Programming Languages as Operating Systems (or Revenge of the Son of the Lisp Machine). Matthew Flatt, Robert Bruce Findler, Shriram Krishnamurthi, Matthias Felleisen. 1999. [Essay]

Growing a Language. Guy L. Steele Jr. 1998. [Essay and Talk]. This essay is based on the keynote address the author gave at the 1998 ACM OOPSLA conference.

How Buildings Learn. Stewart Brand. 1994. [Book, TV Series]. The BBC TV series based on the original book is excellent. It complements the book by delivering the same message in a different medium, and providing additional case studies.

Language Oriented Programming. M. P. Ward. 1994. [Essay].

Compiling with Continuations. Andrew W. Appel. 1992. [Book].

The Originality of the Avante-Garde and Other Modernist Myths. Rosalind E. Krauss. 1985. [Book].

Languages of Art. Nelson Goodman. 1976. [Book].

Notes on the Synthesis of Form. Christopher Alexander. 1964. [Book].

LISP 1.5 Programmer's Manual. John McCarthy et al. 1962. [Book, PDF].

Course in General Linguistics. Ferdinand de Saussure. 1959. [Book].

Syntactic Structures, 1st Edition. Noam Chompsky. 1957. [Book].

Point and Line to Plain. Wassily Kandinsky. 1926. (modern edition, 1979). [Book].
