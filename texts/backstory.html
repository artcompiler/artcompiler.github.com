The Backstory

Art Compiler is my attempt to scratch a long suffered and persistent itch. That itch is best captured by the question "How does language mean?" Quite simply, I'm facinated by how language carries meaning, and what kinds of meaning it carries.

This urge first manifested itself as an interest in computational linguistics, which led to a general interest in language processing and finally to programming language compilers. After a two decade detour into the trenches of compiler writing and programming language design, I find myself with both the skills for and the interest in developing ways to probe language for meaning that can be represented visually.

A program is nothing more than a complex network with it nodes given by the syntax of the program, and its edges given by various context senstive relationships between those nodes. Our job is to discover and represent that network visually so that the program's meaning becomes clear to an observer.

At Art Compiler, we use a collection of compilers and data visualization tools to produce works that combine static and dynamic graphics into an interactive whole. At the core of every visualization is a data base of facts about a program, not unlike what optimizing compilers use. This data base is built and elaborated through a series of phases that synthesize new facts from internal and external sources. Finally, the network represented in the data is rendered as a collection of static images which are manipulated interactively by the users through a dynamic interface.
